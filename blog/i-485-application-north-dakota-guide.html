How to File I-485 in North Dakota: Complete Step-by-Step Guide 2024

Published April 14, 2026 · FormGuard

If you live in North Dakota and are ready to apply for a green card, filing Form I-485 is one of the most important steps in your immigration journey. This complete guide walks you through everything you need to know — from eligibility requirements to what happens after you submit your application. Whether you are sponsored by a family member or employer, understanding the process can help you avoid costly mistakes and delays.

What is Form I-485 and Who Needs It in North Dakota

Form I-485, the Application to Register Permanent Residence or Adjust Status, allows eligible applicants to request a Green Card from inside the United States. In other words, it is the primary form used to apply for lawful permanent residency without leaving the country.

There are different categories of people who use Form I-485 to apply for their Green Card. Though they have different requirements and paperwork, all of them need to file an I-485 to request their permanent residency. Different categories of people, such as family members, employment-based individuals, asylum seekers, and refugees, can file Form I-485.

Form I-485 is used when the person who is applying for their green card is already in the United States. If they are outside the U.S., they may find it more convenient to apply for a green card through consular processing.

For North Dakota residents specifically, it is important to know where your case will be handled. North Dakota is served by the Saint Paul field office located in the State of Minnesota and the USCIS's Kansas City District. The USCIS Application Support Center in Fargo provides biometric services appointments — such as fingerprints, photographs, and electronic signatures — for individuals requesting an immigration benefit with USCIS.

  • Family-sponsored applicants (spouses, parents, children of U.S. citizens)
  • Employment-based applicants with an approved I-140 petition
  • Asylum grantees and refugees eligible to adjust status
  • Special immigrant categories including diversity visa winners

North Dakota I-485 Filing Requirements and Eligibility Checklist

Before filing your I-485, you must confirm you meet the core eligibility requirements. You must be physically present in the United States to file this application. In general, you may not file your Form I-485 until an immigrant visa is immediately available in your category.

USCIS published a new edition of Form I-485 on December 10, 2024. Starting February 10, 2025, USCIS will accept only the 10/24/24 edition of Form I-485 and will reject any older editions. Always download the current form directly from USCIS.gov.

Here is a core eligibility and document checklist for North Dakota applicants:

  • You may apply as the person who directly qualifies for an immigrant category ("principal applicant") or, in some cases, as the spouse or child of a principal applicant ("derivative applicant").
  • Submit a government-issued photo ID such as a passport or driver's license, two color passport-style photographs, and a copy of your foreign birth certificate.
  • Send evidence of your lawful entry to the United States — most people can submit an I-94 arrival/departure record.
  • USCIS requires most family-based applicants to submit Form I-864, Affidavit of Support. The sponsor must meet income requirements under federal poverty guidelines.
  • If you are required to submit Form I-693, Report of Immigration Medical Examination and Vaccination Record, you must submit it with your Form I-485.
  • If you submit any documents in a foreign language, you must include a full English translation along with a certification from the translator verifying accuracy.

Step-by-Step Process for Submitting Your I-485 in North Dakota

Follow these steps carefully to submit a complete and accurate I-485 application from North Dakota:

  • Step 1 — Confirm Eligibility:
  • Before filing a Form I-485 based on a family-sponsored or employment-based preference category, check the USCIS Visa Bulletin page to see if your priority date makes you eligible to apply.
  • Step 2 — Download the Correct Form:
  • Submitting an older edition when USCIS requires a specific edition can result in rejection. Download your form directly from USCIS — not from random websites — and confirm the current edition.
  • Step 3 — Gather All Supporting Documents:
  • Submitting all required initial evidence and supporting documentation at the same time you file Form I-485 may eliminate the need for USCIS to issue a Request for Evidence and may help avoid adjudication delays.
  • Step 4 — Complete the Medical Exam:
  • USCIS requires a sealed Form I-693 completed by a designated civil surgeon. The civil surgeon must sign the form before submission.
  • Step 5 — Determine Your Filing Address:
  • Where you file your Form I-485 depends on your eligibility category. Go to the USCIS Direct Filing Addresses for Form I-485 page to see where you should file your application.
  • Step 6 — Pay the Filing Fee:
  • The cost is $1,440 per person between ages 14 and 78. The fee for children under 14 is $950 (as of Winter 2025).
  • Step 7 — Attend Your Biometrics Appointment:
  • Your biometrics appointment is typically scheduled 4–8 weeks after your receipt, where you will be asked to provide fingerprinting, photographs, and a background check.

Common I-485 Mistakes North Dakota Applicants Make

Even though the form appears simple, many applicants make mistakes that result in their application being rejected. These denials are not always due to major violations — often, they result from small oversights, misunderstandings, or missing information. These mistakes can cost you both money and time.

Here are the most common errors to watch out for:

  • Inconsistent Information Across Forms:
  • If you are filing I-485 alongside related forms like I-765 or I-131, every biographic detail must match — names, addresses, dates, A-Number, and prior history. A small mismatch can trigger extra review or a Request for Evidence.
  • Missing Signatures:
  • Missing signatures remain one of the most common reasons a filing gets rejected. Always confirm you signed exactly where USCIS requires, especially the applicant signature and any interpreter/preparer sections.
  • Wrong Form Edition:
  • USCIS updates forms periodically. Filing an outdated version of Form I-485 that is no longer accepted will result in rejection.
  • Untranslated Foreign Documents:
  • Photocopies of foreign documents that are not translated or do not include official stamps may not be accepted. USCIS expects all documents in a foreign language to be translated into English with a signed certificate of translation.
  • Wrong Filing Fee:
  • Incorrect fees are a common rejection reason. USCIS recommends using official tools to confirm the correct amount — use the USCIS Fee Calculator before submitting.
  • Failing to Maintain Immigration Status:
  • Violating the terms of your nonimmigrant visa — such as working without authorization or overstaying your visa — can jeopardize your I-485.
  • Incomplete Background Disclosure:
  • When filling out your green card application, providing accurate background information is essential. Small, unintentional mistakes could be viewed as lying, leading to denial or possible deportation.

North Dakota USCIS Processing Times and What to Expect Next

Once your I-485 is submitted, the waiting period begins. Processing times vary by category, with family-based adjustment cases averaging about 6–18 months. As of April 2026, USCIS typically takes about 7.5–42 months to process Form I-485 for employment-based Green Card cases.

After USCIS accepts your Form I-485 for processing, they send a receipt notice confirming your application. The mailed notice — Form I-797C, Notice of Action — usually arrives within 2 to 4 weeks.

Here is what to expect at each stage after filing:

  • Receipt Notice: Save this notice carefully.
  • It contains your 10-digit receipt number, which you can use to check your case status.
  • Biometrics Appointment (Fargo ASC):
  • It is important that you make every effort to attend your scheduled biometric services appointment. If you cannot attend, you may request rescheduling through your USCIS online account or by calling the USCIS Contact Center at 800-375-5283.
  • Interview:
  • Within 8 to 14 months, USCIS may schedule an adjustment of status interview. Marriage-based applicants are almost always required to attend, while some other family-based cases may be waived.
  • After the Interview:
  • Upon approval of your I-485 application, you should receive your Green Card, officially granting you lawful permanent residency.
  • If There Are Delays:
  • If your I-485 processing time is outside the normal range, you can submit an "outside normal processing time" inquiry through USCIS.
  • Track Your Case Online:
  • With a USCIS online account, you can sign up for automatic case alerts, see processing times, upload evidence, update your address, and send secure messages.
Filing Form I-485 in North Dakota is a multi-step process that requires careful attention to detail, complete documentation, and strict adherence to USCIS requirements at every stage.
