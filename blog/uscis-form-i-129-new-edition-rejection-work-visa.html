USCIS Rejected My Form I-129 — What the New Edition Deadline Means for Your Work Visa

April 20, 2026FormGuard4 min read

Getting a rejection notice from USCIS on your Form I-129 can feel devastating, especially when your job and legal status depend on a timely approval. A new edition deadline has caught many employers and immigration attorneys off guard, leading to a wave of unexpected rejections. Understanding exactly what changed and why can help you avoid the same costly mistake.

Why USCIS Is Rejecting Form I-129 Submissions Right Now

USCIS has mandated that all petitioners must use the new edition of Form I-129 dated February 27, 2026, beginning on a specific mandatory compliance date. Any submission using an older version of the form after that deadline will be rejected outright, regardless of how accurately it was completed.

This kind of edition transition is not unusual for USCIS, but the timing and scope of this update have created significant confusion. Many employers, HR teams, and even experienced immigration professionals were still using older saved versions of the form without realizing a newer edition had been released.

The rejections are not based on the content of your petition. They are purely administrative, triggered by submitting a form that USCIS no longer accepts. That distinction matters because it means the fix is straightforward, but the delay can still cost you weeks of processing time.

What Changed in the New Form I-129 Edition Dated February 27, 2026

The February 27, 2026 edition of Form I-129 includes several structural and substantive updates that USCIS determined necessary for improved data collection and regulatory compliance.

Key changes in the new edition include:

  • Updated questions related to the beneficiary's prior immigration history and status
  • Revised supplement pages for specific visa classifications, including H-1B and O-1
  • New fields capturing additional employer attestation information
  • Clearer instructions around wage level documentation and worksite details
  • Adjusted formatting that affects how the form is processed electronically

Some of these changes are minor in appearance but significant in how USCIS processes the submitted data. Failing to complete the new fields or submitting outdated supplement pages can result in rejection or a Request for Evidence even after the form edition issue is corrected.

Which Work Visa Categories Are Affected by This Update

Form I-129 is the petition form used for a wide range of nonimmigrant worker visa categories. Because the edition change applies to the entire form, every visa type filed on Form I-129 is affected by this deadline.

The most commonly filed categories impacted include:

  • H-1B: Specialty occupation workers in fields like technology, finance, and engineering
  • H-2A and H-2B: Temporary agricultural and nonagricultural workers
  • L-1A and L-1B: Intracompany transferees in managerial and specialized knowledge roles
  • O-1: Individuals with extraordinary ability or achievement
  • TN: Canadian and Mexican professionals under the USMCA trade agreement
  • E-3: Australian specialty occupation workers

If your employer or immigration attorney filed a petition for any of these categories using an older form version after the mandatory deadline, it is likely to be rejected. Check your filing receipts and confirm which edition was used.

The Most Common Form I-129 Errors That Trigger Rejection or an RFE

Even when petitioners use the correct edition, Form I-129 filings are routinely rejected or questioned due to avoidable errors. USCIS has strict formatting and completeness requirements that leave little room for mistakes.

The most frequent errors include:

  • Using an outdated edition of the form past the mandatory compliance deadline
  • Leaving required fields blank instead of entering N/A where applicable
  • Mismatched information between the petition and supporting documents
  • Incorrect or missing fee amounts, especially when multiple supplements are included
  • Failing to sign and date the petition in the correct locations
  • Submitting the wrong supplement for the specific visa classification
  • Errors in the beneficiary's name, date of birth, or passport number
  • Incomplete employer contact information or federal employer identification number

Each of these errors can delay your case by weeks or even months. In some situations, an RFE forces you to gather additional documentation under a tight response deadline, adding stress and legal costs.

How to Check Your Form Edition and File Without Mistakes

Verifying that you have the correct edition of Form I-129 is the single most important step before submitting your petition. The edition date is printed in the lower left corner of each page of the form.

Follow these steps to protect your filing:

  • Download Form I-129 directly from the official USCIS website at uscis.gov to ensure you have the current version
  • Confirm the edition date reads February 27, 2026 before completing any fields
  • Delete any previously saved or prefilled versions of the form from your computer
  • Read the updated instructions document that accompanies the new edition, as they contain important changes
  • Double-check every required field for completeness and accuracy before submitting
  • Have a second person review the completed form before it is signed and mailed

Frequently asked questions

What happens if USCIS rejects my Form I-129 for using the wrong edition?

USCIS will return your petition along with the filing fees and a rejection notice explaining the reason. You will need to refile using the correct February 27, 2026 edition of Form I-129, which resets the clock on your processing time and may affect your beneficiary's start date or current status.

Where do I find the edition date on Form I-129?

The edition date is printed in small text at the bottom left corner of the form, typically formatted as a date followed by the letter N, such as 02/27/26. Always download a fresh copy directly from the USCIS website rather than using a saved version to ensure you have the most current edition.

Can my employer refile quickly if the petition is rejected for the wrong edition?

Yes, because the rejection is administrative rather than substantive, refiling is usually straightforward as long as you use the correct form edition and correct any other errors. However, the delay can still impact employment start dates, especially for cap-subject H-1B petitions or situations where the beneficiary's current status has a hard expiration date, so speed matters.
